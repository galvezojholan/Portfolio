Introduction

Computing plays an important role in the development of modern science. It helps researchers collect, process, analyze, and interpret data efficiently. Through computing, complex problems in different scientific fields can be solved faster and more accurately.

Today, computing is not limited to computer science alone. It is widely used in formal sciences, natural sciences, and social sciences, making it an essential tool in research, education, and innovation. Understanding the relationship between computing and other sciences allows learners to appreciate how technology supports scientific discoveries and real-world applications

Objective

After studying this lesson, the learners should be able to:

  • Define computing and explain its role in science
  • Identify the different branches of science related to computing
    • Differentiate formal science, natural science, and social science
  • Explain how computing is applied in each type of science
  • Appreciate the importance of computing in scientific research and daily life

Overview: Computing and Other Sciences


Computing and Science

Computing It involves designing and building hardware and software systems, managing and processing information, conducting scientific research using computers, creating intelligent systems, and using technology for communication, entertainment, and information retrieval.
Science It is a broad field which includes everything from the algorithms that make up software to how software interacts with hardware to how well software is developed and designed. It use various mathematical algorithhms, coding procedures, and their expert programming skills to study computer processes and develop new software and systems.
Computing supports science by enabling simulations, data analysis, and accurate measurements, making research more reliable and efficient.

Branches of Science Related to Computing

There are three main branches of science that are closely related to computing:

  • Formal Science: It is an area of study that uses mathematically definable logic systems to generate a result.

    • Examples:
    • Mathematics
    • Logic
    • Theoretrical Computer Science
    • Systems Theory
    • Statistics
    • Cryptography
    • Information Technology
    • Robotics
    • Linguistics

    Formal science uses logical and mathematical systems (like numbers and true/false values) that can be applied to both natural and social sciences through quantitative research.

    Applicatons:
    • Calculators for solving equations
    • Spreadsheet for statistical analysis
    • Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Systems
    • Visual Environments for testing logical models

  • Natural Science:Is an area of study that describes, understands, explains, and predicts natural phenomena by observing and experimenting. It tries to understand the natural world.

    • Two Division Of Natural Science

    • Pure Science: Science to uncover new Information about the world.

    Examples:
    • Biology
    • Chemistry
    • Physics
    • Mathematics
    • Earth Science

    • Applied Science: Uses the discoveries of pure science to create pratical solutions to existing problems.
      - Also known as "Technology"

    Examples:
    • Techonolgy
    • Environmental Science
    • Medicine
    • Electronics
    • Computer Science
    • Engineering

    Computing helps natural scientists collect accurate data, create simulations, and use advanced instruments for research.

    Applications:
    • Electron Microscopes used in biology for inspecting objects
    • CT scans for medical imaging
    • Gamma ray telescopes observe distant heavenly bodies and phenomena.
    • Digital sensorsmonitoring oceanic changes and movement.

  • Social Science: is an area of study which is concerned with society and the relationship between humans.

    • Examples:
    • Anthropology
    • Archaelogy
    • Economics
    • Education
    • Linguistics
    • Geography
    • Psychology
    • Sociology

    Computing aids social scientists in gathering data, analyzing trends, and conducting surveys to understand human behavior and societal patterns.

    Social science uses quantitative and qualitative research but focuses on data from the latter. Social research is more eclectic than scientific research.

    Applications:
    • Polls and census using onling platforms
    • Websites can be educational and the Internet can bring together people of the same lineage yet separated by distance.
    • Social Networks for observing societal trends
    • Laptops and Projectorsfor teaching tools
